Everything packed into the cabinet

Three ways to play, five operations, three difficulties — plus the small stuff that makes a match feel alive.

Solo runs

Clear a ten-question run against the rope on your own. Just you, the arithmetic, and a stubborn length of rope.

Two players, one keyboard

Share a single screen. Each player guards their own side and taps or types the answers from their half of the board.

Versus the CPU

No second human handy? Player 1 answers while the computer works the other end of the rope.

Speed & streak bonuses

Fast correct answers pull harder, and back-to-back correct answers build a streak bonus. Hesitation costs you ground.

Five operations, three tiers

Add, subtract, multiply, divide, or mixed — across Easy, Medium, and Hard timers and number ranges.

Sound, music & haptics

WebAudio blips for hits, misses and wins, plus vibration on supported phones. Sound and music toggle separately, with a graceful fallback where haptics aren't available.

Split-keyboard controls

Player 1 answers with A S D F, Player 2 with J K L ; — four keys per side for the four multiple-choice answers.

Names, avatars & a test

Pick your name and face before the match, then run the sound and haptic test with one button so nothing surprises you mid-game.

High scores & scoreboard

Personal bests and a running scoreboard persist in your browser via localStorage — no account, nothing installed.

Match flow

How a round plays out

From setup to victory in four moves — no manual required.

1

Set up the match

Pick solo, two players, or vs-CPU. Choose an operation and difficulty, then name your player and pick a face.

2

A problem appears with four choices

An arithmetic problem shows up with four answer buttons. Tap the right one — or press its key, A S D F for P1, J K L ; for P2 — before the timer bites.

3

Right answers pull the rope

Each correct answer tugs the rope toward you. Answer fast and string together a streak to pull even harder; wrong answers and timeouts nudge it back.

4

Yank it over the line

Keep the pressure on until the rope crosses to your side. Your best runs land on the scoreboard — then, of course, demand a rematch.
