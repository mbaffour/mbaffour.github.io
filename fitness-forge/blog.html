Why I Built This

Every fitness app wants your data. This one doesn't.

I've tried a lot of fitness apps. Every single one of them wants an account. Then they want your email. Then they want to sync your workouts to a cloud you don't control. Some of them monetize your data. Most of them stop working the moment you lose internet or let your subscription lapse.

I train in a gym with spotty wifi. I don't want to create an account to track a deadlift. I want to open an app, log my session, and close it. Simple.

The goal was a tool that's yours, entirely — running on your device, storing nothing externally, asking nothing of you except your reps.

Fitness Forge is that tool. Everything lives in your browser's localStorage. There's no backend, no sync, no login. You can install it on your phone like a native app and use it in airplane mode. If you clear your browser data, your logs go with it — but that's the trade-off for full local ownership.

Under the Hood

Vanilla JS. Zero build step. Zero dependencies.

Fitness Forge is built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript ES modules — no React, no bundler, no node_modules. It deploys as static files to GitHub Pages and runs instantly anywhere. The only external dependency is Chart.js for progress graphs.

State is managed via a lightweight store module using localStorage for persistence. The service worker enables full offline functionality and installability as a PWA.
