About Me

MBA

Howdy! I'm a PhD student in Microbiology at Texas A&M University, passionate about understanding the intricate world of bacteriophages and how these prolific bacteria-killers unleash their arsenal of tools & tricks to defeat their foes in the ever-raging arms evolutionary race.

My research focuses on lysis inhibition in bacteriophage N4, a phenomenon this phage uses to delay host lysis, enabling it to utilize the host as a phage factory to produce 3000 viral particles per infected cell in a span of 3-6 hours. Through decoding how phages interact with their host and environment, I hope to gain a better understanding of these communication networks and reverse engineer it for biotechnological applications such as high titer phage production for therapy & programmable microbial biofactories. Additionally, I have an interest in environmental phage discovery and have isolated three bacteriophages which I have had the pleasure of characterizing extensively with undergraduates I have been privileged to work with. I'm dedicated to advancing scientific knowledge through rigorous research and innovative approaches to solving the most challenging problems.

As I approach the completion of my doctoral studies, I'm excited to transition into roles where I can contribute to solving the world's most challenging problems and making groundbreaking discoveries along the way.
