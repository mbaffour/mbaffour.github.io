Who I Am

About Me

MBA
4th Year PhD 3 Phages Isolated N4 Specialist

Howdy! I'm a PhD candidate in Microbiology at Texas A&M University, driven by a fascination with how bacteriophages — the most abundant biological entities on the planet — wage molecular warfare against their bacterial hosts. My research sits at the intersection of virology, molecular biology, and genomic engineering.

My primary work centers on lysis inhibition in bacteriophage N4, a remarkable phenomenon whereby the phage strategically delays host cell lysis to transform each infected bacterium into a high-output viral factory, producing up to 3,000 viral particles per cell within 3–6 hours. By decoding these phage decision-making systems, I aim to reverse-engineer them for biotechnological applications including high-titer phage production for therapy and programmable microbial biofactories.

Beyond my primary project, I have a passion for environmental phage discovery. I have isolated and characterized three novel bacteriophages alongside talented undergraduates I've had the privilege of mentoring. As I approach the completion of my doctoral studies, I'm eager to bring my expertise in phage biology, molecular engineering, and computational genomics to roles tackling humanity's most pressing challenges.
